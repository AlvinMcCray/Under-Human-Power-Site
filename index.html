The Expedition

In 2008, I set out on a journey defined by distance, discipline, and resolve—beginning at the sun-scorched border of Mexico and ending at the frozen edge of the Arctic Ocean. Along the way, I sea kayaked 1,400 miles through the Inside Passage, navigating a rugged maze of fjords, tides, and weather where the Pacific meets the continent on its own terms.

That passage was one chapter of a far larger expedition: more than 5,700 contiguous miles traveled entirely under human power. The route followed coastal highways and remote shorelines, threaded through British Columbia's wild waterways, and pressed steadily north beyond the last markers of convenience and comfort. Every mile was earned by hiking, kayaking, canoeing, cross-country skiing, and snowshoeing—no engines, no assistance, no shortcuts.

Just human determination, carried forward one step and one stroke at a time.

That was the quest.
